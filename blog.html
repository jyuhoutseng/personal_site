What is Manga?
One Piece by EIICHIRO ODA
Jujutsu Kaisen by GEGE AKUTAMI
Chainsaw Man by TATSUKI FUJIMOTO
Dragon Ball Super by AKIRA TORIYAMA and TOYOTAROU
SPY x FAMILY by TATSUYA ENDO

Manga is just Japanese comics essentially, however their themes and execution often differ from Western styled comics. Manga is read from right to left and (in the early years) often had panel layouts and page flow that were unseen in the West. Artistically it is also distinct as many share a look that is also not seen too often in Western comics. Personally, I read more manga as a kid so it became my preferred medium for story telling visually and thematically.

Professional Manga Artists!

Akira Toriya's art supplies (left) and Hirohiko Araki drawing timelapse (right)

What I find very interesting is the tools Toriyama used when creating his works. Many of the tools stayed the same such as his pens but there were some that would change later on for obvious reason such as his paint set. It's always very interesting seeing how experienced artists work! Watching Araki draw always makes me want to go draw too, his work "Jojo's Bizarre Adventure" played a major role in inspiring me to create not just drawings but also stories. In a sense, Araki is my artist idol!

My Art Supplies

My personal art supplies are very limited compared to that of Mr. Toriyama's own supplies, due mostly to the differnt mediums we use. I am more of a digital artist due to the convience (and because I like to draw as a hobby) so my techniques and supplies are amateurish at best, but it's still fun to draw manga when I have free time!

These are the pens that I use for drawing. The S9 pen is my go to for quick sketches or if I don't have the other two pens on hand. It is nicely weighted and offeres a nice form factor for drawing and is a reliable default. However, if I have access to the S6 and Staedtler pens I will use them. The S6 pen is great for coloring and making corrections, it doesn't appear well in the photo, but the S6 pen is actually flat unlike regular pens and pencils. The Staedtler pen is great for sketching and I use it almost exclusively for sketching due to its form factor being identical to a real pencil.

Kendo Tournament

Inspiration for the Swordfighting manga!

I have always had an interest in sword fighting and martial arts, however after seeing videos on the many sword forms and mindset of many Japanese swordsmen, I was inspired to start writing a manga centering around mental and physical journey. I wanted to incorperate folklore and a fictionalized but Khmer-inspired culture and setting.

Extreme Tag

Tag as an Extreme / Professional Sport!

Something I never expected to see was a sport based entirely on the game of tag! Watching the highlights of the World Chase Tag tournament was actually really exciting and I instantly wanted to make a manga around that concept. Unfortunately I'm not too good at drawing backgrounds just yet though. But when I get better as an artist I can't wait to start drawing the story out. Hopefully I can make it just as exciting as watching the real sport!
